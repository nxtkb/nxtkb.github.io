## Understanding the Problem

Traditional keyboards force users into a position that can cause:
- Ulnar deviation (wrist bending away from the thumb)
- Internal shoulder rotation
- Neck strain from reaching forward
- Compromised thoracic spine posture

## The Split Keyboard Advantage

### Improved Wrist Positioning

Split keyboards allow hands to return to a "home" position that mirrors a natural handshake, reducing:
- Ulnar deviation by up to 15 degrees
- Wrist extension strain
- Pressure on carpal tunnel pathways

### Shoulder and Arm Benefits

With traditional keyboards, shoulders are forced together in an unnatural position. Split keyboards:
- Allow shoulders to assume their natural, slightly wider position
- Reduce internal rotation of the shoulders
- Enable the arms to extend straight from the torso

### Neck and Back Support

When using a compact split keyboard:
- Users have more space to position their monitor at proper distance
- Reduced forward head posture is often observed
- Greater flexibility in desk positioning

## Research-Backed Findings

Multiple studies have indicated that split keyboards can:
- Reduce risk of repetitive strain injuries
- Improve typing comfort during extended sessions
- Potentially increase typing speed over time as muscle memory develops

## Making the Transition

### Initial Adjustment Period

Most users experience an adjustment period when transitioning to split keyboards. This is normal and expected. Studies suggest it takes 2-4 weeks to feel comfortable, and up to 8 weeks for complete adaptation.

### Tips for Success

- Start slowly and gradually increase usage
- Focus on proper positioning and setup
- Be patient with yourself during the learning process
